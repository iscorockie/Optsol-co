2026 Tax Changes Every Business Owner Needs to Know

DO
David Osei, CPA, TEP
March 2, 2026 · 8 min read

Overview: What's Changing in 2026

The new fiscal year brings a raft of changes to corporate and personal tax legislation that will directly impact how businesses plan, report, and pay their taxes. As qualified CPAs, our team has reviewed every update and distilled the most critical points for business owners.

💡 Pro Tip

Start your mid-year tax review now. Many of these changes have retroactive provisions — acting early could unlock significant savings.

Key Corporate Tax Changes

The most significant update is the revised corporate tax rate structure, which introduces a tiered system for SMEs with annual turnover below $5M. Qualifying businesses may see effective rate reductions of up to 3 percentage points.

  • New capital allowance rules allow immediate expensing of qualifying assets up to $1M
  • R&D tax credit expanded to include software development for in-house tools
  • Enhanced relief for businesses employing new graduates and apprentices
  • Transfer pricing documentation requirements tightened for cross-border transactions

Depreciation Rule Overhaul

Perhaps the most practically impactful change is the overhaul of depreciation schedules. The previous declining balance method for several asset classes has been replaced with straight-line depreciation, which may affect tax timing for capital-intensive businesses.

⚠️ Action Required

Review your fixed asset register now. Businesses with significant plant and equipment may need to recalculate deferred tax positions before filing.

Personal Tax Implications for Business Owners

Owner-operators and directors drawing dividends will face an updated dividend tax credit calculation that reduces the effective benefit of dividend distributions versus salary in certain income brackets. Our advisors can model the optimal remuneration structure for your situation.

AI in Accounting: How Technology is Reshaping Finance

JM
James Mutua, CPA, CA
February 18, 2026 · 6 min read

The AI Revolution in Finance

Artificial intelligence is no longer a future prospect for the accounting profession — it's here, and the firms and businesses embracing it are already gaining measurable advantages in speed, accuracy, and insight depth.

💡 Key Insight

Firms using AI-assisted reconciliation report a 60–80% reduction in manual processing time, freeing accountants to focus on high-value advisory work.

Where AI is Making the Biggest Impact

  • Automated bank reconciliation and transaction categorisation
  • Anomaly detection for fraud prevention and audit trail review
  • Predictive cash flow modelling and scenario analysis
  • Intelligent document processing for invoice and receipt management
  • Natural language queries on financial data for non-finance users

What This Means for Your Business

The shift doesn't eliminate the need for qualified accountants — quite the opposite. AI handles the repetitive, high-volume tasks, while CPAs and Chartered Accountants apply their expertise to interpretation, strategy, and client guidance. The human element remains irreplaceable.

Your Audit Readiness Checklist: 10 Steps to Prepare

AN
Amara Nwosu, CA, CISA
January 30, 2026 · 5 min read

Why Preparation Matters

A well-prepared audit is faster, less disruptive, and typically produces better outcomes for the business. Our audit team has compiled the essential checklist every finance department should work through before auditors arrive.

✅ Checklist

Print this checklist and work through it with your finance team 4–6 weeks before your audit date.

  • 1. Reconcile all bank accounts — Ensure statements match ledger balances
  • 2. Review accounts receivable aging — Identify and document any disputed invoices
  • 3. Confirm fixed asset register — Verify physical existence and condition of major assets
  • 4. Prepare board minutes — Collate all board resolutions for the period
  • 5. Organise contracts & agreements — Lease agreements, loan documents, service contracts
  • 6. Review related-party transactions — Document any transactions with connected parties
  • 7. Reconcile intercompany accounts — If group entities exist, ensure they balance
  • 8. Update cut-off procedures — Ensure revenue and expenses fall in the correct period
  • 9. Prepare tax schedules — Have current-year tax calculations ready
  • 10. Review prior-year audit points — Demonstrate you've addressed prior findings

Cash Flow Strategies That Actually Work in 2026

NK
Naledi Khumalo, CA, CFA
January 12, 2026 · 7 min read

The Cash Flow Challenge in 2026

Rising input costs, tighter credit conditions, and shifting payment terms are putting cash flow under pressure for businesses of all sizes. But with the right strategies in place, many businesses are not only surviving — they're building stronger financial foundations than ever before.

💡 Pro Tip

Build a 13-week rolling cash flow forecast. It's the single most powerful tool for avoiding cash crunches and making confident investment decisions.

Top 5 Cash Flow Strategies

  • Invoice early, collect faster — Review credit terms and implement early payment incentives
  • Build a cash buffer — Target 8–12 weeks of operating expenses in liquid reserves
  • Optimise inventory levels — Use just-in-time principles where possible
  • Negotiate extended payables — Work with suppliers to align outflows with your cash conversion cycle
  • Use dynamic discounting — Offer customers flexible payment options that improve your yield
